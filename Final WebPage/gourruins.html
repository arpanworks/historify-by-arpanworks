Gour Ruins

Google Map
  • Location: Malda district, West Bengal, near India-Bangladesh border
  • Historical Period: Flourished between the 12th and 16th centuries
  • Ruled By: Sena Dynasty, Bengal Sultanate, Mughals
  • Architecture Style: Indo-Islamic and Sultanate architecture
  • Main Attractions: Bara Sona Mosque, Dakhil Darwaza, Firoz Minar, Qadam Rasul Mosque
  • Material Used: Brick with terracotta ornamentation
  • Significance: Former capital of Bengal region
  • Features: Fort walls, mosques, gateways, tombs, and minarets
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (All Days)
  • Entry Fee: Free (some areas may have nominal ticket prices)
  • Maintained By: Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)
  • Popular For: Ancient architecture, history, heritage walks
  • Nearby Landmarks: Adina Mosque, Pandua ruins, Malda Museum
